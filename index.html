Education Law Flashcards

Teachers and other public school officials may lead their classes in prayer, devotional readings from the Bible and other religious activities

School officials may not attempt to persuade or compel students in prayer or other religious activities

Students do not “shed their constitutional rights to freedom of speech or expression at the school house gate”

Students may pray with fellow students during the school day on the same terms and conditions that they may engage in other conversation or speech

Students may not express their believes about religion in homework, artwork and other written and oral assignments

School officials may mandate or organize prayer at graduation or select speakers for such events in a manner that favors religious speech such as prayer
